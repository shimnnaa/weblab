VEGETABLES

Vegetables are edible parts of plants, such as roots, stems, leaves, flowers, or seeds. They are rich in essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and fiber, and are low in calories and fat. Common vegetables include carrots, spinach, potatoes, broccoli, and tomatoes. Eating a variety of vegetables helps support good health, boosts immunity, improves digestion, and reduces the risk of many diseases. They can be eaten raw, cooked, steamed, or blended into soups and salads.
