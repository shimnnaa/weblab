onam

Onam is the most important and widely celebrated festival in the Indian state of Kerala. It is a harvest festival that marks prosperity, joy, and cultural heritage. Onam is celebrated by people of all religions and communities in Kerala.

time of onam celebration

Onam is celebrated in the Malayalam month of Chingam (August–September) and lasts for 10 days, starting with Atham and ending with Thiruvonam, the main day of the festival.

The Legend of King Mahabali

The festival is based on the legend of King Mahabali, a kind and generous ruler who was loved by his people. According to mythology, Lord Vishnu took the form of Vamana (dwarf) to curb Mahabali’s growing power. Before sending him to the underworld, Vishnu granted him a wish to visit his people once a year – this visit is celebrated as Onam.

Pookalam (Floral Decorations)

The Onam Sadhya is a grand vegetarian feast served on banana leaves. It includes a variety of dishes such as:

  • Rice, sambar, avial, thoran, olan, pachadi, and more
  • Payasam (sweet dessert)

Traditional Dances and Cultural Programs

Various folk dances and art forms are performed during Onam, such as:

  • Thiruvathirakali (women’s group dance)
  • Pulikali (tiger dance)
  • Kathakali (classical dance-drama)
  • Kummattikali (mask dance)

Onakkodi (New Clothes)

People wear new traditional clothes during Onam. It is a custom for elders to gift new clothes, known as Onakkodi, to younger members of the family.

Home Decorations and Festivities

Homes are cleaned and decorated with lamps, flowers, and pookalams. People visit temples, meet relatives, and participate in community events.

Significance of Onam

  • Symbolizes the golden era of prosperity under King Mahabali
  • Represents cultural unity, as it is celebrated by people of all religions
  • Highlights Kerala's agricultural richness and cultural traditions
    • Onam is a festival that goes beyond religion and celebrates values like unity, generosity, and happiness. It brings people together and showcases the rich traditions of Kerala. It is not just a festival, but a cultural celebration of life itself.
