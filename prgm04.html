onam

Onam is a major annual festival celebrated in the Indian state of Kerala. It marks the harvest season and honors the legendary King Mahabali, whose reign is believed to be a golden era of prosperity and happiness. The festival typically lasts ten days and features traditional music, dance, elaborate feasts called Onam Sadhya, boat races, flower decorations (Pookalam), and cultural events. Onam symbolizes unity, joy, and the rich cultural heritage of Kerala.

diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a major Hindu festival celebrated across India and many other countries. It is known as the "Festival of Lights" and symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. The festival usually lasts five days and includes lighting oil lamps (diyas), decorating homes, bursting fireworks, exchanging sweets and gifts, and performing religious rituals. Diwali celebrates various legends, including the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana. It is a time of joy, family gatherings, and new beginnings.

holy

Holi is a vibrant and colorful Hindu festival celebrated mainly in India and Nepal, known as the "Festival of Colors" or the "Festival of Love." It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. During Holi, people throw colored powders and water at each other, dance, sing, and enjoy festive foods. The festival also celebrates the playful love between the Hindu god Krishna and Radha. Holi is a joyful occasion that brings people together, breaking social barriers and spreading happiness.
